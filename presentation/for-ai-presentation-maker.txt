SLIDE 1 — TITLE
---
Title: CalmCue
Subtitle: Neurodivergent-friendly voice chat that reduces sensory overload via real-time audio UX adaptation.
Footer: UPenn Hackathon · Sensory Overload Shield

SLIDE 2 — HOOK
---
Title: The big idea
Body: Voice chat shouldn't feel like a sensory assault.
Subtext: We fix the sound, not the words.

SLIDE 3 — THE PROBLEM
---
Title: The problem
Body: Voice calls and meetings feel chaotic for many people—especially those who are neurodivergent or sensitive to auditory overload.
Key point: It's not the words. It's the dynamics.
Bullets:
- Overlapping voices → hard to parse who said what
- Shouting spikes → physically overwhelming
- Rapid interruptions → anxiety and fatigue
- No recap → "What did I miss?" after zoning out

SLIDE 4 — WHAT WE'RE NOT DOING
---
Title: What we're not building
Key point: This is NOT toxicity or content moderation. We don't classify or police what people say.
Body: We focus only on:
Bullets:
- Audio dynamics (loudness, overlap, interruptions)
- Turn-taking UX (cues, leveling, ducking)
- Accessibility (captions, focus-mode summaries)
Footer: So everyone can stay in the conversation without sensory overload.

SLIDE 5 — THE SOLUTION
---
Title: The solution: CalmCue
Body: A real-time voice room that adapts the experience—not the content.
Bullets:
- Dynamic leveling — keep each speaker near a comfortable volume
- Overlap ducking — when 2+ people talk, soften non-dominant speakers
- "One speaker at a time" — gentle cue + optional sound cue
- Live captions — who said what, with timestamps
- Focus mode — "What did I miss?" summary (bullets, decisions, action items)
- Learning loop — your feedback adjusts sensitivity for next time

SLIDE 6 — HOW IT WORKS
---
Title: How it works
Column 1 — Real-time pipeline:
- WebRTC voice room (3–4 people)
- Web Audio: gain + compression per speaker
- Audio analysis: loudness, voice activity, overlap and interruption detection
- Shield actions: leveling, ducking, UI overlay
Column 2 — Intelligence:
- Transcription (Modulate Velma) → captions + speaker labels
- Summaries via Airia OpenAI Gateway → "What did I miss?"
- Telemetry in Postgres → Lightdash for metrics
- ThresholdAgent: learns from feedback, proposes updated settings

SLIDE 7 — SPONSOR TECH
---
Title: Sponsor tech we use
Bullets:
- Modulate (Velma Transcribe) — robust transcription in messy audio
- Airia (OpenAI Gateway) — Focus Mode summarizer
- Lightdash — metrics dashboards over session telemetry
Footer: Plus WebRTC, Web Audio API, Node, Next.js, Prisma, Discord bot for room links and shield settings.

SLIDE 8 — DEMO
---
Title: See it in action
Body: In the demo we show:
Bullets:
- Two or more browsers in one room with chaotic multi-speaker audio
- Overlay: "Overlap detected" + dynamic leveling in real time
- User toggles: Shield ON/OFF, sensitivity, captions, one-speaker cue, Focus mode
- "What did I miss?" → 3-bullet summary via Airia
- Feedback buttons (Too aggressive / Too weak) → next session uses learned thresholds
- Lightdash: session telemetry and improvement metrics
Callout: Captions + leveling make overlapping talk manageable.
