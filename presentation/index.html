CalmCue

Neurodivergent-friendly voice chat that reduces sensory overload via real-time audio UX adaptation.

UPenn Hackathon · Sensory Overload Shield

Voice chat shouldn’t feel like a sensory assault.

We fix the sound, not the words.

The problem

Voice calls and meetings can feel chaotic for many people—especially those who are neurodivergent or sensitive to auditory overload.

Not the words. The dynamics: overlapping voices, sudden volume spikes, rapid interruptions, and no clear “who’s speaking.”
  • Overlap → hard to parse who said what
  • Shouting spikes → physically overwhelming
  • Interruptions → anxiety and fatigue
  • No recap → “What did I miss?” after zoning out

What we’re not building

This is NOT toxicity or content moderation. We don’t classify or police what people say.

We focus only on:

  • Audio dynamics (loudness, overlap, interruptions)
  • Turn-taking UX (cues, leveling, ducking)
  • Accessibility (captions, focus-mode summaries)

So everyone can stay in the conversation without sensory overload.

The solution: CalmCue

A real-time voice room that adapts the experience—not the content.

  • Dynamic leveling — keep each speaker near a comfortable dB
  • Overlap ducking — when 2+ people talk, soften non-dominant speakers
  • “One speaker at a time” — gentle cue + optional earcon
  • Live captions — who said what, with timestamps
  • Focus mode — “What did I miss?” summary (bullets, decisions, action items)
  • Learning loop — your feedback adjusts sensitivity for next time

How it works

Real-time pipeline

  • WebRTC voice room (mesh, 3–4 people)
  • Web Audio: GainNode + DynamicsCompressor per speaker
  • AudioWorklet: RMS → dB, VAD, overlap & interruption detection
  • Shield actions: leveling, ducking, UI overlay + optional earcon

Intelligence

  • Transcription (Modulate Velma) → captions + speaker labels
  • Summaries via Airia OpenAI Gateway → “What did I miss?”
  • Telemetry in Postgres → Lightdash for overlap, interruptions, improvement
  • ThresholdAgent: learns from feedback, proposes bounded threshold updates
